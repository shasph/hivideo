In our browsing interface, we create a poster layer that displays the video chapter in slides and the spoken material in the video in a poster format. The poster layer can be interactively navigated to other layers by click the content such as title, text, and figure.

To allow users to associate the video with the slide in a convenient way, we designed the video layer as illustrated in video. Users can change the number of slides to be displayed. And the slides on the right will change in sync with the video as it plays. A click operation on the slide allows users to fast-forward the video to the specific segment they want to see.

To fulfill the usage case (as we discovered in the preliminary study), in which users choose to read text rather than video, we designed the slide layer. Users can view the detail information of the specific slide. In addition, users can navigate to other layers using a simple click operation.

In contrast to previous video browsing interface, we adopt Responsive Web Design so that users can achieve better experience when accessing it with mobile browsers.
